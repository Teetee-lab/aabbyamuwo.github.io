About Me

I am Titilayo Amuwo, however, I like to be called Abigael. I specialize in the fields of Data Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Cloud computing. I received my Associate degree from Rufus Giwa Polytechnic in 2008 and Bachelors from the University of Lagos in 2015, where I studied Business Administration and Management. After my associate degree, I landed my first job as a Security Trading Operation Specialist at Stanbic IBTC Bank. After working as a Banker for 6 years, I continued to work in the field of Accounting and Finance for 5 years which means that I not only have experience as an Operation specialist but also in Accounting where I get to analyze data and share reports with stakeholders.
Since my teenage years, I have always had a passion for technology and innovation; aside from that, I am the go-to person in my family and among friends when it comes to information technology. This has always been my passion; however, things change along the line and I follow the family tradition of going into business and management so, I pursued another degree instead. While working as an accountant, I realized that we use data every day and the same data is used to build a dashboard where we can share valuable information with our clients about their finances. I wanted to learn more about the designs and software, and use it to develop my skill set; hence, I decided to merge my previous experience using data and my passion for innovation by pursuing another career in Information Technology, data science to be precise.
Since transitioning into a data science career in 2021, I have been contracted to work at companies and also had the opportunity to Intern at one of the leading cybersecurity companies in Palo Alto. With that being said, I am a versatile Data science professional with experience in compiling, transforming, and analyzing complex information using data science skills. As a Data scientist, I have helped develop advanced visualization concepts and implemented machine learning algorithms that allow businesses to make better decisions that yield high returns on investment. My background allows me to provide insights that drive business decisions and I am confident in my ability to use my technical and interpersonal skills to make an impact in any organization.
Outside of work, I enjoy playing with my daughter, drawing, reading and volunteering at Women Who Code Global as a community member or collaborating at other non profit organizations. Currently, I am learning how to build and develop a Large Language model to assist organizations evolve in the financial market while they make a better decision that will increase return on Investment. I am certain that I can facilitate positive change, and impact outcomes in any organization.

Projects

Soccer Match Betting

This project contains my approach to building a predictive model with the aim to minimize cost while maximizing the return on investment on soccer betting.

Landmark Tagging for Social Media

In this project, I address the problem of discernable landmark images by building a CNN-powered app to automatically predict the location of an image based on any landmarks depicted in the image.

Scone Unlimited Image Classification Project

In this project, I developed an image classification model that can automatically detect which kind of vehicle delivery drivers have in order to route them to the correct loading bay and orders. The project focuses on assigning delivery professionals who have a bicycle to nearby orders and giving motorcyclists orders that are farther away.

Bike Ride Sharing System

The competition is about forecasting the use of bike ride-sharing systems in Washington DC. Bike-sharing systems are a means of renting bicycles where the process of obtaining membership, rental, and bike return is automated via a network of kiosk locations throughout a city.

Tableau Data Analysis

The page consists of different Tableau workbooks, where I analyzed and visualized different public data.

Flower image Classification Project

In this project, I have implemented a method of transfer learning on a Vgg16_bn pre-trained model to train a dataset of flower categories. The output of the pre-trained model is then used to build a command line application that takes the image of a flower as input and predicts its category.
